Step 3

Fetch CrossRef percentiles

Each paper is matched against CrossRef, then its citation count is ranked against all other articles in the same journal × year. Same algorithm as Citation Compare. Papers from are not scored (too few citations accumulated to be meaningful). Books, book chapters, conference papers, and theses are identified from the citation format (or directly from the BibTeX entry type) and marked NA — the journal × year framework doesn't apply.

How to read this. Percentile = where the paper ranks among all articles in the same journal × publication year, by CrossRef citation count. A 78th percentile means more cited than 78 % of articles in that journal that year. The matched title is shown beneath each citation — entries flagged with ⚠ may be wrong matches and worth double-checking on Citation Compare directly. Books and chapters are identified heuristically from the citation format (presence of “In” before a book title, a closing “Place: Publisher” pattern, or editor markers) and shown as NA across all fields. If a citation is misclassified, edit it in step 2 and re-run.
